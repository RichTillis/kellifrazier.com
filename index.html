Educational Philosophy

All students have a right to an equitable free and appropriate public education with differentiated instruction to meet individual learning needs. The environment in which students are taught should have a safe, encouraging atmosphere. Education should focus on developing the whole child, teaching academic, social, and emotional learning for all. An inherent culture of discourse, critical thinking, and independence should exist. Learning should be made relevant to students’ lives and application of skills practiced in multiple settings, not just in a classroom. All individuals, students, staff, and families, should be encouraged to reflect on practice and growth. To lead meaningful change, reflection should be on all levels of learning and guided by the use of data to make an informed plan for change.

Furthermore, I believe that every student has the ability to learn and be successful. In order to create an atmosphere conducive to learning, students must feel comfortable taking risks. Therefore, I strive to foster a school and classroom community where students and families feel safe and supported. In addition, families and students each bring a unique set of skills and experiences to the school and classroom and learn best in varying ways due to different learning styles, interests, and preferences. I embrace these differences and use them to strengthen learning experiences in my school and classrooms. I set high expectations for each and every one of my staff members as well as students, while providing the structure and guidance necessary to achieve success.

Leadership Philosophy

As an educational leader, I develop and communicate a clear vision for all stakeholders and provide a path to accomplishing the mission of Gateway Polytechnic Academy. This includes an ability to express and inspire all stakeholders to meet the needs of all students. It is essential each student is met with cultural competence and equity in learning while being shown compassion. All students’ learning should focus on academic, social, and emotional development. All stakeholders need to be passionate about the learning process not solely focused on outcomes. The vision and mission identifies who the individuals are and what each one wants to develop into as staff, students, parents, families, and community. As a leader, I inspire commitment from staff and also develop trust from all stakeholders. It is important to do this with transparency and in an easy to understand language defining ourselves with our values, attitude, vision and measure of success.

Promoting Student Learning and Professional Growth

All children can learn when provided opportunities and instruction which meet their individual learning styles and needs. Instruction provided using research-based strategies and differentiated for individual student needs will allow students to find success. It is a leader’s responsibility to build capacity for all learners in problem solving issues in society, contributing to the well-being of humanity, and develop opportunities for self-fulfillment. A school has to promote learning that is relevant to the lives of its students. Learning and knowledge have to be applicable to what students experience daily. Instructional leaders need to provide supervision and evaluations which encourage teachers to be classroom leaders; teachers are part of a system that has to have a belief all students are “our kids” not just “my students.”

Data collection and progress monitoring are essential tools in learning. Analyzing data gives guidance on areas of growth and need by identifying strengths and weaknesses. Data collection should minimize impact on direct instruction while assisting teams in pinpointing topics for professional development. Professional development should relate directly to student and staff performance and requires opportunities to learn, practice, get feedback, and implement skills and strategies. Teachers need to be exposed to different types of assessment and encouraged to use them as a means to determine learning while believing they can utilize them and take risks just as professionals should be encouraging students. Technologies can be used in teaching and learning as a means of making learning and information more relevant to how students experience their world today.

School Management and Organization

School leaders should encourage all learners to be in school daily and provide a safe, nurturing environment. School safety and effective management are essential in creating an environment where students want to attend. Individual actions and attitudes create a powerful force for school climate. A crucial decision as a leader is selecting professionals who will provide support and lead the school community with a determination focused on the vision, mission, and safety of all stakeholders. It is the leader’s responsibility to develop commitment and loyalty to the vision with words and actions. A belief in learning with risk-taking should be reinforced for students and staff while being empowered to explore and use their new knowledge and skills in new and innovative ways. All stakeholders are responsible for problem solving and to develop continuous school improvement.

Vision for School and Community Relations

Schools are an integral part of the community in preparing students as relevant members of society. The interactions between a school and community are at the core of a belief that it takes a village to raise and develop our young members which is the core purpose of a school and the community. The community’s connection and involvement with the school is imperative in supporting student success. School leaders at all levels and community stakeholders have an interest in developing a relationship built on transparency and trust. As a leader, it will be a priority to establish visibility, communication, and involvement with the community. A school leader has to value, not in what ways, but in all ways the relationship between the school and community stakeholders promotes student growth, achievement, and success as learners and members of society.

Ethical Leadership

A leader must demonstrate and model clear ethical standards for the entire school community. A leader’s ethical compass should always face “north;” everyone has an ability to advance, do better, learn, adapt, and grow. If a leader follows the compass, improvement and learning follow. A school leader should not ask others to do what she or he wouldn’t do themselves. A belief that all stakeholders should advocate and defend social justice for all members of its community and society as a whole should be ingrained in the culture of the school. All stakeholders deserve privacy and it should be protected, not marginalized or made dependent on situations. All stakeholders should be given the inalienable right of self-determination; individuals have an ability to make decisions for themselves. A school leader should continuously promote and communicate that the concepts of fair, equal, and equitable are all different, but that all stakeholders are given these without question.
